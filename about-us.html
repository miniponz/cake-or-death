Cara Ponzini

Cara is a batholith, exposed over an area of about 285 km2 in the Washington Cascades and is among the earliest intrusions in the Cascade plutonic arc. A recent study combined fieldwork, mineralogy and geochemistry to characterize Cara and compare her with other Cascade batholiths, the goal being to identify spatial and/or temporal patterns in Cara's chemistry over the past 35 Million years.

Emily Creamer

This shy, quiet creature is almost exclusively nocturnal, and very seldom seen. Its scales not only act as a deterrent, they also protect it from predators. As the days shorten, and the nights grow longer, this perplexing creature will utilize its devious tactics to avoid being seen. It can melt into the environment expertly, and any unsuspecting person can easily mistake it for just another rock. Perhaps it will make a mistake, and deviate from its usual routine.
